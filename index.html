Your Property in Context

Updated property profile

Public records appear to understate the home, and we have updated our understanding of the property using the seller-supplied appraisal figures showing 2,026 square feet above grade, 908 finished square feet below grade, 2,934 finished square feet total, and 3,047 square feet total. That short clarification is important because square-footage inconsistencies in this pocket can skew CMAs and make an otherwise well-positioned listing look off when it is not.

The real opportunity here is not just size. It is lifestyle. A cul-de-sac setting near Cheese Ranch open space, standout curb appeal, a 3-car garage, newer driveway, replaced roof, lifetime-warranty windows, updated HVAC, hardwood floors, fresh paint and carpet, upgraded baths, and a true outdoor entertaining package give this home a stronger buyer story than a basic 1990 house. That is the argument for pushing toward the top end of the neighborhood price-per-foot range rather than settling at the middle.

Strengths

  • Updated appraisal size makes the subject more competitive with the better closed comps in the neighborhood.
  • Cul-de-sac placement near Cheese Ranch open space is a real lifestyle positive that buyers will understand.
  • Curb appeal is a standout feature and helps this home compete at the top end of neighborhood price per square foot.
  • Major capital items are already addressed: roof in 2015, all windows in 2015, furnace and 4-ton AC in 2011, new driveway in 2025.
  • Recent cosmetic refresh helps first impression: new paint on main and upper level in 2026, new carpet upstairs in 2026, hardwoods on the main level in 2020.
  • Outdoor living package is meaningful, not cosmetic: built-in kitchen, new decking, gas flagstone fire pit, and oversized hot tub.

Things to watch

  • The fourth bedroom is a stretch today because it functions as a home theatre. We can market it as 4 beds, but some buyers will want a truly ready bedroom and an appraiser may view that count conservatively.
  • Public-record size needs a clean explanation so buyers and appraisers understand why our square-footage story differs from county data.
  • Buyers will compare this home closely with 5584 and 5543 Chambray Court, so we need to respect those same-pocket anchors while arguing for the lifestyle premium.
  • If the market does not respond quickly near 800k, we should adjust fast rather than let the listing sit and become stale.

Pattern Callout

What the comps say about strategy

The pattern is straightforward. The best sales above 800k were generally larger, had more bedrooms or baths, or carried a stronger premium on lot, size, or presentation. The most relevant same-pocket sales support a value center in the upper 700s. Fast or near-list results prove that right pricing works. Longer exposure or meaningful discounts show what happens when sellers try to start at the ceiling instead of earning it.
